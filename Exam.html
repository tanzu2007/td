Curriculum/Courses offered/Assessment and Promotion Criteria.

Conduct of Examination:

In line with the government policy of conducting the examination in fair and just manner, all the school examination in Kuzshugchen Middle Secondary School is been conducted and coordinated by the examination committee.

Promotion and Detention Policy:

Promotion and Detention meeting will be held to discuss the progress of the students with class teacher, subject teacher, and examination committee headed by the Academic Head. Class teachers will prepare the report card without any errors. Promotion to the next grade will be based on the following criteria:

Class PP---III

  1. The subjects offered are Dzongkha, English and Mathematics
  2. For the promotion to the next higher grade, every student of the classes PP—III must score 40 percent and above in the subjects offered at their level.
  3. The student should have 90% attendance for the promotion to the next higher grade
  4. Promotion would be done on the basis of formative and summative assessment.

Class IV----VI

  1. The Subjects offered are Dzongkha, English, Mathematics, S/Studies and Science. For the promotion to the next grade, a candidate must pass with 40% in the major subjects (Dzongkha, English and Mathematics).
  2. A Candidate failing in any one of the minor subjects (Social Studies and Science) could be promoted to the next grade, if he/she does not score below 30%.
  3. The student should have 90% attendance for the promotion to the next higher grade. The Promotion would be done on the basis of the formative and summative assessment

Class VII—VIII

  1. The subjects offered are Dzongkha I, Dzongkha II, English I, English II, Mathematics, History & Civics, Geography and Science. For the promotion to the next higher grade:
  2. For the promotion to the next grade, a candidate must pass with 40% in the major subjects (Dzongkha and English)and pass mark (40%) in any three remaining subjects with a minimum grade C in SUPW.
  3. A Candidate could be promoted to the next grade if he/she does not score below 30% in one minor subjects and a pass mark (40%) in the remaining subjects.
  4. The students should have 90% attendance to be promoted to the next higher grade.
  5. Promotion would be done on the basis of the formative and summative Assessment.
    6. summative Assessment.

Class IX—X

  1. The subjects offered are Dzongkha I, Dzongkha II, English I, English II, Mathematics, HCG (History & Civics, Geography) and Science (Physics, Chemistry and Biology) and the sixth subject (Economics, E.V.S and Computer Application).
  2. For the promotion to the next grade, a candidate must pass with 40% in the major subjects (English and Dzongkha) and pass mark (35%) in any three remaining subjects with a minimum grade C in SUPW.
  3. A candidate failing in any one of the remaining subjects could be promoted to the next grade if he/she doesn’t scores below 30% in any one of the minor subjects.
  4. The student should have 90% attendance for the promotion to the next higher grade
  5. Promotion would be done on the basis of the formative and summative Assessment.

Awards and Recognition to Students:

To promote and motivate students to strive for excellence, students who excel are recognized by awarding the following category of awards and certificates.

  1. Certificates of Honors: Award to students achieving an aggregate of 90% or more in the Home exams.
  2. Certificates of Excellence: Award to students achieving an aggregate of 80% to 89.9% in the Home exams.
  3. Overall Grade Topper Certificate: Award to the overall topper from each grade across the section.
  4. Certificate of Leadership: Award to the office bearers in appreciation of the responsibilities shouldered if the student deserve only.
  5. Best student of the year: Award to the best all-rounder student from class PP - X, judged on five criteria (academics, discipline, co-curricular activities, leadership and volunteerism). The criteria will be measured on the scale of 1 to 5; one being poor and five as outstanding.

Note: The certificate will be signed by the School Principal, Academic Head Student’s Affairs and the Class Teacher for the subject toppers.
